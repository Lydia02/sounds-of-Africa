Davido

Biography

Click to read the full biography

David Adedeji Adeleke, popularly known as Davido, is a Nigerian-American singer, songwriter, and record producer who has become one of the most prominent figures in African music. Born on November 21, 1992, in Atlanta, Georgia, Davido was raised in Lagos, Nigeria, where his love for music began. He made his debut in the Nigerian music scene in 2011 with the single "Back When," featuring Naeto C, but it was his hit song "Dami Duro" that catapulted him to stardom.

Davido's unique ability to blend Afrobeats, Afropop, and international sounds has made him a global sensation. His debut album, *Omo Baba Olowo* (2012), was an instant success and showcased his knack for creating infectious melodies. Since then, Davido has released several chart-topping albums, including *Son of Mercy* (2017), *A Good Time* (2019), and *A Better Time* (2020), featuring collaborations with international stars like Chris Brown, Nicki Minaj, and Lil Baby.

A leader in the Afrobeat movement, Davido's music is characterized by his vibrant energy, catchy hooks, and meaningful lyrics that resonate with fans worldwide. His groundbreaking single "Fall" became the longest-charting Nigerian pop song in Billboard history, and his anthem "If" cemented his place as a household name in Africa and beyond.

Beyond music, Davido is a philanthropist and cultural ambassador. He is the founder of DMW (Davido Music Worldwide), a record label nurturing some of Africa's top talents, such as Mayorkun and Peruzzi. His philanthropic efforts, including a $600,000 donation to Nigerian orphanages in 2021, highlight his commitment to giving back to his community.

With over 20 awards to his name, including BET and MTV Africa Music Awards, Davido continues to shape the global perception of African music, making him one of the most influential artists of his generation.
