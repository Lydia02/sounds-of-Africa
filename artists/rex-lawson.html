Rex Lawson

Biography

Click to read the full biography

Rex Jim Lawson, also known as Cardinal Rex, was a renowned Nigerian highlife musician and composer. Born in 1935 in Kalabari, Rivers State, Nigeria, Rex Lawson revolutionized African highlife music with his soulful voice and emotional compositions. His music, often performed in Kalabari, Igbo, and Pidgin, resonated with audiences across West Africa.

Some of his iconic songs, such as "Sawale," "Jolly Papa," and "So Ala Temen," remain timeless classics, embodying the essence of African highlife. His career was tragically cut short when he died in a car accident in 1971, but his legacy endures as a cornerstone of Nigerian music history.

Musical Legacy

Rex Lawson's influence on highlife music cannot be overstated. His compositions, deeply rooted in African traditions, paved the way for future generations of musicians. Lawson's ability to convey powerful emotions through his music has earned him the title of a pioneer in the genre.
