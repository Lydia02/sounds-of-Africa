Mbalax

Mbalax is Senegal's most popular music genre, characterized by its fusion of traditional Wolof rhythms with jazz, funk, and Afro-Cuban sounds.

History

The genre emerged in the 1970s, led by artists like Youssou N'Dour, who modernized traditional sounds for a global audience.

Key Characteristics

  • Complex percussion using Sabar drums
  • Fusion of African and Western influences
  • Emotionally expressive lyrics

Prominent Artists

  • Youssou N'Dour
