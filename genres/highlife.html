Highlife

Highlife is a genre deeply rooted in Ghanaian culture. It is known for its blend of traditional African music with jazz and brass band sounds.

History

Originating in the early 20th century, Highlife evolved from brass band music played at colonial gatherings. It became a cultural phenomenon in West Africa, celebrating life and love.

Key Characteristics

  • Use of guitars and brass instruments
  • Lyrics often in local languages
  • Danceable and celebratory rhythm

Prominent Artists

  • E.T. Mensah
  • Rex Lawson
